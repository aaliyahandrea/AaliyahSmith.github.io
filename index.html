Brain Stroke Classification Model

According to the CDC, in the United States, a brain stroke occurs every 40 seconds, and in every 3.5 minutes someone dies of one. A brain stroke is caused by a blockage in the brain's blood supply or when a brain vessel bursts. We can prevent 80% of strokes from occurring according to UCLAHealth. Some of the controllable risk factors are smoking, heart disease, high blood pressure, and physical inactivity.

When going to the doctor/hospital in case of an emergency or yearly check-up, your history is usually discussed with a physician. The beforementioned risk factors are usually asked in the initial survey after being admitted or measured. I believe that by looking at the symptoms/history and comparing it to brain stroke occurrences you will be able to classify a case using the k-nearest neighbor's algorithm.
